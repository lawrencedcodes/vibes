The Golden Ticket Myth

"Tremendous things are in store for you... I shall be happy to refill your cupboard with whatever you want."
— Willy Wonka, 1964

Tech is a battlefield. Not a fairy tale.

"Society, friends, family, and tech Twitter sometimes make this industry look like a golden ticket. You graduate, you find a ticket, and a truck drops off success for the rest of your life." "Guess what? That’s a children’s book. If you want to be an extra in someone else's fairy tale, go ahead. I’m trying to be the main character."

The Two Principles of Career Wealth


🪙

1. Daily Deposits

The intentional, consistent work you do every single day.

📈

2. Compound Interest

The outsized, exponential rewards you get back over time.

1. Play the Hand You're Dealt

You don't control your starting line.

STORY TIME (CHRIS KOHN): "Spring 2024, Google I/O in Mountain View. Chris is a top-notch spades player, but he lost badly because he took on a brand new person as his partner. On our drive to San Francisco, he dropped this wisdom: In life, you just have to play the hand you’re dealt." "Some of you had to hustle, scrape, and claw just to sit in these seats today."

Then, Stack the Deck.

Turn disadvantages into assets.

Network aggressively. Build community.

STORY TIME (MY TRANSITION): "In 2006, I was a middle-aged Black man working in the restaurant business in Oxford, MS. Two years later, I landed my first web dev job. Now, I travel the world." "How? I used age to my advantage—I told interviewers I wanted a home, not to job-hop. I networked at every meetup. Every person you meet is a Daily Deposit. The network you build today might land you a job in five years. That is Compound Interest."

2. Execution is the Game-Changer

"Showing up is half the battle... execution is the other half."

Develop a relentless bias for action.

"Showing up is just walking to the bank. Execution is making the deposit. A perfectly polished resume means nothing if no one sees it. Rewards are for those who write, build, and ship."

No Pressure = No Progress

Where there is no friction, there is no growth.

STORY TIME (TOPMATE MENTEE): "A mentee on Topmate told me: 'Lawrence, I’m just trying to find the easiest, quickest way into tech.' I told them: 'Easy come, easy go.'" "Don't fake the work. Writing the code and solving the hard problems—that is the deposit. The reputation you build from being a problem-solver is the compound interest."

3. The Pursuit of Mastery

Treat coding as a craft.

STORY TIME (KUNG FU & MULAN): "Growing up in Jackson, MS, I watched Kung Fu theater every Saturday. Drunken Master, 36th Chamber. The best scenes? The training montages." "Don't settle for mediocrity. Become obsessed with being the best. Be the person marching up the mountain carrying the water buckets like Mulan. When you do that, others say, 'If they can do it, I have to do it.' That inspiration is your compound interest."

4. Mindset as a Weapon

"Whether you think you can or you can't, you're right."
- Henry Ford
"Your mindset is the account that powers all the others. But positive thinking isn't ignoring reality. If you walk through a rose bush chanting 'There are no thorns,' you're going to come out bleeding."

Acknowledge Reality.
Choose Action.

Systemic barriers exist. Doubters exist.

Push through anyway.

"Positive mindset isn't ignoring the fact that Black engineers make up only 3% of big tech, or that HBCUs are underfunded." "It says: I don't have to 'break into' anything. I will prepare, get my reps in, and make it anyway." "A resilient mindset is the ultimate interest-bearing account. When you face a setback—a layoff, a failed project—you bounce back faster. That preserved momentum is what separates good careers from legendary ones."
